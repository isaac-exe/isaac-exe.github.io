About

Hey, I'm Isaac.

I'm a filmmaker, artist, and web developer based in my hometown Madison, WI, but I bounce between NYC and LA as well. I graduated from Carleton College in 2020 with a degree in Cinema and Media Studies, and am currently working at Mortarr as a Frontend Developer. I also freelance as a producer and am coordinating photo shoots for CitizenM at the moment.

You can take a look at my latest film, Let the Good Times Bowl on Vimeo. It's a short fiction narrative film that's inspired the American and French New Wave, The Big Lebwowski (1998), and the amazing people around me I call my friends.

In my free time I keep myself busy by writing screenplays, short stories, essays, and music related stuff for Outside Indie. I've been known to DJ and produce house music, and am working on directing and producing a screwball dark comedy releasing in December 2021. I also create and curate art in the NFT space, and am excited to tell everyone about a secret project I'm working on.

Right now I'm reading The Pale King by David Foster Wallace, and have been listening to a lot of Slow Pulp, a band from Madison.

If you're interested in my bad opinions on movies, check out my letterboxd.
